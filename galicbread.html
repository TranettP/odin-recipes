Cheessy Garlic Bread

Cheesy garlic bread is a popular type of bread that is flavored with garlic and melted cheese. It typically starts with a loaf of bread, such as French or Italian, which is sliced and then spread with a garlic butter mixture made from butter, minced garlic, and often herbs like parsley or oregano. The bread is then topped with grated cheese, such as mozzarella or parmesan, and baked until the cheese is melted and bubbly. The result is a crispy and savory bread with a rich garlic flavor and gooey melted cheese on top. Cheesy garlic bread is often served as an appetizer, side dish, or accompaniment to soups, salads, or pasta dishes. It is a favorite comfort food for many people and is easy to make at home.

Ingredients

  • 1 Loaf French bread
  • 1/2 cup salted butter softened
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 3 garlic cloves minced
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 cup Colby jack cheese shredded
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese shredded
  • fresh chopped parsley

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees. Cut your French bread loaf lengthwise and place on top of a baking sheet.
  2. In a small bowl combine softened butter, garlic powder, minced garlic, and Italian seasoning. Spread evenly on each side of the garlic bread. Top with Colby Jack and mozzarella cheese.
  3. Bake in the oven for 12-15 minutes until butter is melted an bubbly. You can also broil the top for 1-2 minutes at the end to give it a more crisp top.
  4. Garnish with fresh chopped parsley if desired.
